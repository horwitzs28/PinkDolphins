What is a pink dolphin?

A pink dolphin is a dophin breed that has pink skin! I know it sounds like something you might've drawn in elementary school, but they do exist.

A pink dolphin is a type of dolphin that naturally has pink-looking skin. When they are young, pink dolphins normally have grey skin, and their pink skin develops over time. Their final color is mainly based on their diet, behavior, sunlight exposure, and capillary placement.

Fun fact: when dolphins are excited, they can flush a brighter pink! Can you think of another animal that does this?


These pink dolphins are sadly endangered. They are considered a vulnerable species in certain areas with dams and contamination of rivers and lakes.

What's so cool about these dolphins?

Well for one, they are pink! But there are other things too.

These naturally occuring pink dolphins only live in rivers. They can be called "Amazon Pink River Dolphins" because they mainly live in the amazon river. These dolpins are really smart, with a brain capacity that is 40% larger than that of a humans!

Fun fact: pink dolphins can grow up to 8 feet long and weigh over 450 pounds. They have bigger brains than other dolphins!
